The unifying idea

A video = a timeline of scenes

Every creator you sent + Tella point to the same model. Each scene has a layout (where your cam + screen sit), captions, overlays (pop-ups), and optionally segmentation (text behind you / object emphasis). Finding · Tella + Nate Herk + Jack

The layout-switching system, decoded

Tella models a video as a base layout + timed layout segments on a timeline, with Smooth (animated move+resize) or Hard cut transitions. The creators confirm the real-world grammar:

  • Cam-as-corner-PIP over full-screen content (Nate Herk ~60–70%): rounded-rect, ~18–32% wide, corner opposite the active UI.
  • Framed scene (Jack): screen + bottom-left squircle cam floated on a colored gradient backdrop, ~6–8% inset, soft shadows — he gave a full JSON schema.
  • Layout presets (Tella): cam-only, screen-only, corner-bubble (9-point grid + S/M/L), 50/50 split, TV-presenter, camera-backdrop, squircle borders.
Maps directly onto what we built: add a layoutTrack to the EditPlan and a <LayoutTrack> layer to SpeakerOverlayScene that positions two synced video layers (your cam + your screen) and tweens their {x,y,w,h} between segments.
