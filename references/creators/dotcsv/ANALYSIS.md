## Templates observed

### Template A — Podcast cutdown with B-roll cutaway
**Reels using it:** `DVYwdtTlEdv`, `DWQyY8ZjboN`, `DXZsUU3iniP`, `DXhKM_4FTiw`, `DX4UnX1k0nY`, `DYdAMBHBkAC`, `DYe0INikbZm`, `DYmnLaBCb3x`, `DXmQeRIiCi2` (majority of the scrape).

**Visual structure:**
- Audio: Carlos talking-head from a podcast (often Lex-Fridman-style two-shot).
- Picture: alternates between (a) the talking-head shot and (b) full-bleed B-roll cutaways (Pentagon aerial, food close-ups, archival news clips, datacenter photography).
- Caption: bold white sans-serif, 1–2 words per beat, bottom-third, no background plate. Word-by-word reveal synced to spoken word.
- No watermark, no breadcrumb, no accent color.

**Motion grammar:**
- Picture cuts hard on punctuation (no transitions).
- Captions pop in word-by-word (similar in spirit to our EditorialCaption, but with NO active-word color highlight — just on/off).

**Map to our typology:** closest to **Stream E #5 PodcastClipCutdown** — already on our long-tail roadmap, low priority for Sprint 1. Skip for now.

### Template B — Movie-clip meme with translated caption
**Reels using it:** `DUql5MADn8C` (Will Smith "estas son albóndigas" clip).

**Visual structure:**
- Audio: original movie audio (Spanish dub or original English with translation).
- Picture: licensed/scraped movie footage, full bleed.
- Caption: bold white sans-serif, single-line translation pinned center-low.

**Motion grammar:**
- Zero animation on Carlos's end — pure repost with caption.

**Map to our typology:** **not applicable** — meme repurposing is outside our brand grammar. We don't recreate this.

### Template C — Event repost (co-authored)
