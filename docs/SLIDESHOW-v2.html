R6 transcripts · biggest insight

"Easing is the single biggest 'looks AI' tell"

10 of 10 YouTube transcripts fetched (English; Spanish 429-rate-limited but English coverage was sufficient). The most-cited motion-design rule across multiple tutors:

"If there's one motion design technique that applies to everything, it's easing... that's what gives objects their wobble, their bounce, and makes rigid lifeless animations like this feel more natural and real."
— Mt. Mograph

What this means for us: we currently use raw interpolate() with linear default + a few inline bezier literals + house springs. Action for Wave 4:

  • Build src/timing/easing.ts with named curves (outCubic, inOutCubic, outBack, outQuint, outExpo)
  • Sweep all compositions to use named easing — no more inline bezier literals
  • Add easing: z.enum([...]).default("inOutCubic") to schemas
  • Promote "easing-first" rule into CLAUDE.md Coding Conventions

Other 4 distilled insights: specific-vs-vague-prompt tradeoff, parenting/null-object pattern (React: SceneController + HookFrame/SceneBody/CTAFrame), visual-inspiration-first, always-load-best-practices-skill. Full synthesis at docs/research/wave-1/R6-transcripts-synthesis.md.
