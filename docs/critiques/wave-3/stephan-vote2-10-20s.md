## 5. Replicate-this-template spec

If we were to add ONE template to our library based on what's in this 10s, the highest-value pickup is the **"split-screen UI demo + talking head with seam-caption"** template (17.59-20.00s+). Here's the spec:

### Template name: `split-screen-ui-talkhead.json`

**Aspect**: 9:16 vertical (1080×1920).

**Layout**:
- Top region: 0% → 45% of canvas height. Hosts a screen recording / app capture / web video. May be a still image or a scrolling video.
- Bottom region: 45% → 100%. Hosts speaker footage (talking head, locked-off, medium framing).
- Hard horizontal seam at 45% — no soft mask, no rounded corners. Both regions are full-bleed.

**Caption layer**:
- Anchored exactly on the seam (y ≈ 45% — caption baseline straddles the line).
- Text: bold sans-serif (Inter Black 700-900 weight), white fill, 1-2px dark drop-shadow (offset ~2px y).
- Font size: ~64-72px equivalent at 1080w (chunky but not screen-wide).
- Caption swaps every 0.3-0.8s, one 1-3 word phrase at a time. Snap-on, no fade.
- No background pill — text floats over both panels.

**Top-panel behavior**:
- Should support either a static screenshot OR a scrolling capture.
- If scrolling, scroll speed ~50-100 px/sec (slow enough to read).
- Mouse cursor visible — helps it feel "live", not staged.

**Bottom-panel behavior**:
- Locked-off camera. No pan, no zoom. Natural background.
- Speaker is medium-close (chest-up), centered-ish, head in upper third of bottom panel.

**Why this template**:
- It's the workhorse of "I built X, here's what it does" content. Maps directly to our use cases (showing Remotion templates, showing pipeline output).
- The seam-caption placement is genuinely novel and reads as a unifying visual element across the two panels. Most split-screen templates push captions to top or bottom — this puts them on the join, which makes the viewer's eye scan BOTH halves naturally.
- Production cost is low: we already have screen-recording capability and the speaker footage is plain webcam.

### Bonus template (lower priority): `keyword-pedestal.json` (the Peaky pattern, 11-12s)

Two-line stacked caption where the top line is a small rotating descriptor and the bottom line is a giant, fixed anchor word/acronym. Top swaps with the audio; bottom stays put for the duration of a "concept block". Cheap to implement, high comprehension density — good for explainer beats where you have a sticky term and want to riff on it.
